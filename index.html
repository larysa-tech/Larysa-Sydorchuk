Welcome to Ukraine

The capital of Ukraine

Kyiv is one of the oldest cities in Eastern Europe and the capital of Ukraine. With more than 1,500 years of history, it is a city rich in culture, architecture, and traditions. Kyiv is located on the beautiful Dnieper River and is famous for its golden-domed churches, green hills, and vibrant modern life.

Famous places in Kyiv

  • Kyiv Pechersk Lavra (Monastery of the Caves)
  • Saint Sophia Cathedral
  • Maidan Nezalezhnosti (Independence Square)
  • Andriyivskyy Descent

Fun facts about Kyiv

  1. Kyiv has more than 350 km of green areas and parks.
  2. Kyiv’s chestnut trees are one of the city’s main symbols.
  3. Andriyivskyy Descent is often called the “Montmartre of Kyiv”.
  4. The city is home to over 1,000 churches and cathedrals.
  5. Kyiv hosted the Eurovision Song Contest twice (2005 and 2017).

Kyiv Metro

The Kyiv Metro opened in 1960 and is the third oldest metro system in the former Soviet Union. It is famous for its deep stations and beautiful Soviet-era architecture. The station Arsenalna is officially the deepest metro station in the world, reaching 105 meters underground.
